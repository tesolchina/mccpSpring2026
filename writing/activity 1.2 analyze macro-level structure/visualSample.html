Section 1: Introduction ~800 words
Move 1: Establishing Territory
Centrality Claims: Personalized anatomical digital twins are "key" in medicine, sports science, biomechanics, computer graphics
"Creating personalized anatomical digital twins of humans is key in fields such as medicine, sports science, biomechanics, and computer graphics."
Strong interdisciplinary appeal. Uses present tense for timeless significance.
Move 2: Establishing Niche
Gaps Identified: Expensive medical imaging, time-consuming segmentation, lack of non-invasive methods
"To the best of our knowledge, the precise 3D prediction of these layers inside the body, given only the outer body surface, is a novel problem that has not been tackled in the literature."
Specific, researchable gap with academic hedging ("to the best of our knowledge").
Move 3: Occupying Niche
Solution: HIT method addresses three main challenges with implicit tissue modeling
"Our approach, Human Implicit Tissues (HIT), addresses these challenges."
Clear positioning with enumerated challenges and acronym introduction.
↓
Section 2: Related Work ~600 words
Move 1: Thematic Overview
Scope: Body composition prediction from various inputs (scans, silhouettes, images)
"Motivated by prior work on the prediction of body composition from 3D scans, silhouettes, or images, we go further..."
Immediate differentiation ("we go further") establishes contribution.
Move 2: Critical Analysis
Themes: Anatomic models, anatomy inference, datasets with systematic critique
"Several methods create avatars with soft tissue deformation... None of these are validated against clinical data."
Strong critical analysis identifying validation gaps in existing work.
Move 3 & 4: Gaps & Synthesis
Gaps: Lack of clinical validation, limited tissue types, no volumetric inference
Gaps established through systematic critique rather than explicit statements.
↓
Sections 3-4: Methods & Data ~1500 words
Technical Specificity
Dataset Creation: MRI segmentation, SMPL fitting, tissue classification
Method: Multi-space implicit modeling with deformation fields
"HIT defines four R³ spaces. A point x_m in the original MRI space corresponds to x_p in the posed space, x_β in the shaped space, and x_c in the canonical space."
High technical precision with mathematical formalism appropriate for CS audience.
↓
Section 5: Experiments ~800 words
Empirical Validation
Metrics: Dice scores, volume differences, ablation studies
Generalization: New shapes and poses testing
"Table 1 shows that the HIT Dice scores are significantly better than the Chance baseline."
Rigorous quantitative evaluation with appropriate baselines and ablation studies.
↓
Section 6: Discussion & Conclusion ~400 words
Synthesis & Impact
Contributions: Novel problem formulation, dataset, method
Limitations: Validation scope, tissue specificity
Future Work: Structural losses, individual features
"HIT introduces the new problem of inferring the human tissues inside of a body from a surface observation only."
Balanced discussion with transparent limitations and clear future directions.

Imitation Framework for Future Papers

Structural Elements to Adapt:

  • Multi-space Formalism: Clear definition of coordinate systems and transformations
  • Modular Architecture: Breaking complex systems into understandable components
  • Ablation Studies: Systematic evaluation of system components
  • Dataset Contributions: Detailed data creation methodologies

Rhetorical Strategies:

  • Problem Novelty: "To the best of our knowledge" + specific technical gaps
  • Technical Positioning: Clear differentiation from related work
  • Clinical Relevance: Connecting technical contributions to real-world applications

Quality Indicators:

  • Mathematical Rigor: Formal definitions and loss functions
  • Empirical Validation: Multiple evaluation metrics and baselines
  • Transparency: Open dataset and code availability
  • Scalability: Generalization to new conditions
