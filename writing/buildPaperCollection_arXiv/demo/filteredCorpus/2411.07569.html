We wish to submit an original research manuscript entitled “Towards Automated Model Design on Recommender Systems” for consideration by ACM Transactions on Recommender Systems (ToRS). This research extends our WebConf’23 paper, “NASRec: Weight Sharing Neural Architecture Search for Recommender Systems” and contains many new research efforts. We confirm that the additional work is original and has not been published elsewhere, nor is it currently under consideration for publication elsewhere.

This manuscript demonstrates an Automated Machine Learning (AutoML) paradigm for designing recommender models to improve personalization and benefit social good. We incorporate two important new insights from a model-hardware co-design perspective as an extension of our WebConf’23 paper on Neural Architecture Search for Recommender Systems (NASRec). Section 3.2 discusses the potential of combining NASRec models with Processing-In-Memory hardware, pushing the realization of model efficiency from a co-design perspective. We perform new experimental evaluations to support the claims made in the aforementioned sections. Section 6.1 proposes the study of structured pruning on AutoML-crafted models with state-of-the-art performance and reveals the critical solution that takes effect. Overall, we present a full-stack solution to realize recommender system designs with full design automation in this submitted manuscript. We attach the original WebConf’23 paper as supplementary material for reference.
