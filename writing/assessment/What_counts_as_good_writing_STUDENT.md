# Structured Reflection Questions

Based on the assessment rubrics and course requirements, please consider the following questions:

## Structured Reflection Questions

### 1. Research Community Expectations

**Question 1.1:** What are the specific expectations for good writing in your research community? Consider:
- What do your target journals or conferences value most in published papers?
- What writing style, tone, and structure do model papers in your field typically follow?
- What makes a paper stand out as "excellent" in your discipline?

**Question 1.2:** Identify 2-3 model papers from your target journals/conferences. What specific features make these papers effective? Consider:
- How do they structure their introductions and literature reviews?
- What linguistic features and rhetorical moves do they use?
- How do they synthesize and critique existing literature?

---

### 2. Task Achievement (Based on Assessment Rubrics)

**Question 2.1:** Research Background and Objectives
- How do you currently explain your research background? What makes an explanation "extremely clear" versus "generally clear"?
- What challenges do you face in describing your research focus and objectives succinctly?

**Question 2.2:** Literature Synthesis and Critical Evaluation
- What does it mean to "skilfully paraphrase, synthesise and critically evaluate" literature in your field?
- How do you currently approach synthesizing multiple sources? What could you improve?
- What does "critical evaluation" look like in your discipline? Give an example of how you would critique a methodology or finding.

**Question 2.3:** Research Gap and Significance
- How do you currently identify and articulate research gaps? What makes a gap "clearly delineated"?
- How do you demonstrate the significance of your research? What makes significance "succinctly highlighted"?

**Question 2.4:** Sources, Citations, and Referencing
- What citation style does your field use? What are common mistakes you need to avoid?
- How do you ensure "adequate and effective use of sources" without over-citing or under-citing?

---

### 3. Organisation (Based on Assessment Rubrics)

**Question 3.1:** Logical Flow and Cohesive Devices
- What cohesive devices (transition words, linking phrases, etc.) are commonly used in your field?
- How do you ensure your research story flows logically from introduction to literature review?
- What makes a research narrative "exceptionally clear" and "cohesive"?

**Question 3.2:** Paragraph Structure and Topic Development
- How do you structure paragraphs in academic writing? What makes a paragraph effective?
- How do you ensure each paragraph connects to your overall research aim?

**Question 3.3:** Rhetorical Moves
- Review the rhetorical moves for Introduction (Establishing Territory, Identifying Niche, Occupying Niche) and Literature Review (Thematic Overview, Critical Analysis, Research Gaps, Conclusion). Which moves do you find most challenging? Why?

---

### 4. Language Range and Accuracy (Based on Assessment Rubrics)

**Question 4.1:** Grammar and Sentence Structure
- What "advanced grammatical structures" are valued in your field? (e.g., complex noun phrases, passive voice, conditional structures)
- What grammatical errors do you commonly make? How can you address them?

**Question 4.2:** Lexical Resources and Word Choice
- What makes vocabulary use "precise" in academic writing? Give examples of vague vs. precise word choices in your field.
- How do you expand your academic vocabulary? What discipline-specific terminology do you need to master?

**Question 4.3:** Spelling, Punctuation, and Word Forms
- What are common spelling/punctuation errors in your writing? How do you catch them?
