Section I: Introduction ~900 words
Move 1: Establishing Territory
Centrality Claims: MCS leverages crowd wisdom for sensing data collection. Applied in diverse fields: environment detecting, traffic monitoring, health care, social welfare. Blockchain addresses single point of failure.
"MOBILE crowdsensing (MCS) has gained considerable interest and adoption in past years, which leverages the crowd wisdom for sensing data collection."
Strong centrality claims with practical application examples. Emphasis on blockchain's role in addressing single point of failure.
Move 2: Establishing Niche
Data-driven Gaps: 72 uncompleted requests (>2 weeks) in Mturk, 44.4% >1 month. Technical Gaps: (1) Privacy leakage in transparent execution, (2) Off-chain (untrusted) vs. On-chain (high cost), (3) Truth discovery challenges (privacy, proof, overhead)
"However, these studies mainly focus on incentive mechanism design, ignoring some of the security problems brought when executing incentive mechanisms on the blockchain. First, the workers' information which is used as the input of the mechanism can be private... Second, the correctness and integrity of the incentive mechanism should be ensured wherever the mechanism is executed."
Highly systematic gap identification with enumeration (First, Second) and specific technical limitations. Dual consideration of privacy and correctness/integrity.
Move 3: Occupying Niche
Solution: Two-stage incentive scheme (blockchain + TEE). On-chain public contracts + off-chain private contracts enabled by TEE. Three-fold contributions: efficiency, robustness, privacy preservation.
"Our scheme achieves high efficiency both on-chain and off-chain without huge computation costs and overhead first. Second, stateless design of TEE requires that TEE only performs execution faithfully without using the secure storage, ensuring the robustness of our design. Third, our design is both secure and privacy-preserving..."
Strong positioning with explicit three-fold contribution structure. Addresses potential limitations of TEE adoption before presenting solution.

Section II: Related Work ~800 words
Move 1: Thematic Overview (Three Categories)
Categories: (A) Incentive Mechanism for MCS (traditional → blockchain-based), (B) Truth Discovery for MCS (traditional → blockchain-based), (C) TEE-Assisted Blockchain Applications (general → crowdsensing-specific)
Excellent thematic organization covering all aspects of the proposed system. Each category addresses a different component of the two-stage scheme.
Move 2: Critical Analysis
Limitations: Privacy issues ignored, incomplete recruitment progress, high computation cost, communication overhead, precision sacrifice. Pattern identification: "All schemes aforementioned suffer from: (1) High computation cost; (2) Much communication overhead; (3) Precision sacrifice"
"Note that both [13] and [45] provide incentive mechanisms with comprehensive consideration. They suffer from the following disadvantages: (a) Incomplete recruitment progress. (b) High computation cost and communication overhead using several cryptographic methods."
Strong critical analysis with enumerated limitations. Pattern of identifying common limitations across multiple schemes.
Move 3 & 4: Gaps & Synthesis
Gaps: Online incentive mechanism not studied in blockchain-based MCS. System-level gaps: frequent TEE usage brings back centralization. Need for new architecture.
"Besides, the online incentive mechanism has never been studied in blockchain-based crowdsensing. Existing online algorithms cannot be adopted directly since realizing online incentives within a blockchain architecture would be very challenging..."
Gaps clearly established with specific technical and system-level challenges. Clear positioning statement sets up need for proposed architecture.
↓
Section III: System Model ~900 words
III-A: Crowdsensing Model
Formal Definitions: Roles (Requesters, Workers, Blockchain, Distributed Storage, Incentive mechanism, Truth discovery algorithm). Online reverse auction model. Two-stage scheme: (1) Online incentive mechanism for worker recruitment, (2) Truth discovery for data aggregation.
Clear formalization with explicit two-stage structure. Formal mathematical notation for online auction problem and truth discovery algorithm.
III-B: Adversary Model & III-C: Design Goals
Adversaries: Malicious requesters, malicious workers, malicious TEE nodes. Goals: Efficient (short computation time, high throughput), Robust (against failures and attacks), Privacy-preserving (three privacy goals).
Comprehensive adversary model including TEE nodes as potential adversaries. Three-fold design goals (efficient, robust, privacy-preserving) matching contribution structure.
III-D & III-E: Assumptions & Privacy Goals
Assumptions: Majority honest security, TEE security (enclave integrity/confidentiality), threshold availability (k out of l+1 nodes). Privacy Goals: Sealed private information, worker participation anonymity, data aggregation privacy.
Clear security assumptions including TEE-specific requirements. Explicit privacy goals separate from design goals.
↓
Section IV: Design Details ~2500 words
IV-A: Design Overview
Architecture: Client (requester/worker), TEE node (active/inactive), TEE colony, IPFS node. Phases: Initialization, Task release, Bidding, Recruitment decision, Data submission, Data aggregation, Payment.
Comprehensive architecture overview with clear component descriptions. Phase-by-phase protocol structure.
IV-B: Initialization Phase
Contracts: General contracts (on-chain), Medium contracts (on-chain, trigger private contracts), Private contracts (off-chain, TEE enclave). Robustness Mechanisms: Recovery mechanism (threshold cryptography, stateless design), Hash check mechanism (prevent input tampering).
Strong emphasis on robustness mechanisms. Key sharing for recovery, hash check for integrity. Stateless TEE design prevents single point of failure.
IV-C: Recruitment Decision Phase
Algorithm: OBIM (Online Budget-feasible Incentive Mechanism). Runs inside TEE enclave. Properties: Computationally efficient, individually rational, truthful (cost- and time-truthful), budget feasible. Theorem 1: OBIM satisfies all desired properties.
"Theorem 1: OBIM is computationally efficient, individual rational, truthful, and budget feasible."
Detailed algorithm presentation with theoretical guarantees. Online setting with time-based recruitment decisions.
IV-D: Data Aggregation Phase
Algorithm: CATD (Confidence-aware Truth Discovery). Runs inside TEE enclave. Steps: Data decryption, weight estimation, truth estimation, iterations until convergence, worker evaluation (high/low).
Comprehensive truth discovery algorithm integrated with privacy preservation through TEE execution.
↓
Section V: Analysis of Privacy Goals ~800 words
Attack Prevention
Free-riding & False-reporting: Prevention through truthful mechanism (OBIM), worker evaluation in truth discovery, automatic payment with deposit. Malicious TEE Nodes: Remote attestation verification, hash check mechanism, timeout-based replacement.
Comprehensive attack analysis covering multiple adversary types. Systematic analysis of prevention mechanisms.
System Properties & Privacy Goals
System Properties: No single point of failure (blockchain + stateless TEE + threshold cryptography), no trusted third party. Privacy Goals: Sealed private information (encryption with TEE public key), worker participation anonymity (encrypted data), data aggregation privacy (TEE execution).
Strong security analysis covering both system-level properties and privacy goals. Clear connection to design goals from Section III.
↓
Section VI: Performance Evaluation ~1400 words
VI-A: Experimental Setup
Platform: Hyperledger Fabric v2.3 + Intel SGX. Metrics: Time cost of contracts, time cost of phases, OBIM performance (objective function, overpayment ratio), data aggregation performance (RMSE, MAE).
Comprehensive experimental setup with TEE platform. Multiple evaluation dimensions covering both stages of the scheme.
VI-B & VI-C: Time Cost Analysis
Results: On-chain contracts <35ms, TEE contracts <300ms even with 200 transactions. Phase execution times acceptable for practical use. Comparison shows superior performance to cryptographic methods.
Strong efficiency evaluation showing practical feasibility. Comparison highlights advantages over cryptographic approaches.
VI-D & VI-E: Algorithm Performance
OBIM: Objective function increases with workers/tasks, overpayment ratio ~160%, execution time acceptable. Data Aggregation: CATD outperforms Mean/Median baselines, RMSE/MAE metrics, efficient runtime (<1s for ≤6000 data points).
Comprehensive algorithm evaluation with quantitative metrics. Dual focus on incentive mechanism and truth discovery performance.
↓
Section VII: Conclusion ~300 words
Synthesis & Impact
Summary: Two-stage incentive scheme (blockchain + TEE) with online incentive mechanism and truth discovery. Theoretical security analysis and experimental validation. Contributions: System architecture, robustness mechanisms, two-stage scheme, theoretical guarantees.
Clear summary emphasizing three-fold contributions (efficiency, robustness, privacy) with theoretical and empirical validation.

Imitation Framework for Future Papers

Structural Elements to Adapt:

  • Multi-category Literature Review: Three thematic categories mapping to system components (Incentive → Truth Discovery → TEE)
  • Data-driven Motivation: Use specific statistics or observations to motivate problems (72 uncompleted requests, 44.4%)
  • Enumerative Contribution Claims: Three-fold contribution structure (efficiency, robustness, privacy)
  • Two-stage Problem Formulation: Clear separation of incentive mechanism and data aggregation stages
  • Robustness Design: Explicit mechanisms for handling failures (recovery mechanism, hash check mechanism)

Rhetorical Strategies:

  • Systematic Gap Identification: Use enumeration (First, Second) for multiple gap dimensions
  • Limitation Addressing: Address potential limitations of adopted technologies (TEE) before presenting solution
  • Pattern Identification: Identify common limitations across multiple schemes ("All schemes aforementioned suffer from...")
  • Data-driven Claims: Use specific statistics to motivate problems

Quality Indicators:

  • Architectural Completeness: Detailed protocol design for all system phases with robustness mechanisms
  • Robustness Mechanisms: Explicit mechanisms for handling failures and attacks (recovery, hash check)
  • Comprehensive Evaluation: Multiple metrics covering different aspects (efficiency, effectiveness, accuracy)
  • Quantitative Results: Specific performance metrics with tables and figures
  • Theoretical Security: Formal analysis of security and privacy properties
  • Two-stage Evaluation: Separate evaluation of each stage (incentive mechanism + truth discovery)
