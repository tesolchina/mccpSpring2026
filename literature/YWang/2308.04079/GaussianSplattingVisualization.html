Section 1: Introduction ~1000 words
Move 1: Establishing Territory
Centrality Claims: Radiance Field methods have "revolutionized" novel-view synthesis
"Radiance Field methods have recently revolutionized novel-view synthesis of scenes captured with multiple photos or videos."
Strong centrality claims with practical applications emphasis.
Move 2: Establishing Niche
Gaps Identified: Speed-quality trade-off, no real-time rendering at 1080p
"For unbounded and complete scenes (rather than isolated objects) and 1080p resolution rendering, no current method can achieve real-time display rates."
Highly specific, quantifiable gap with concrete constraints (1080p, ≥30 fps).
Move 3: Occupying Niche
Solution: Three key elements: 3D Gaussians, optimization, fast rendering
"We introduce three key elements that allow us to achieve state-of-the-art visual quality while maintaining competitive training times and importantly allow high-quality real-time (≥ 30 fps) novel-view synthesis at 1080p resolution."
Clear positioning with enumerated contributions and quantifiable outcomes.

Section 2: Related Work ~1500 words
Move 1: Thematic Overview
Scope: Traditional reconstruction, neural rendering, point-based rendering
"We first briefly overview traditional reconstruction, then discuss point-based rendering and radiance field work, discussing their similarity."
Clear thematic organization with emphasis on connections between paradigms.
Move 2: Critical Analysis
Themes: Traditional reconstruction (MVS issues), Neural rendering (speed-quality trade-off), Point-based (equivalence to NeRF)
"While the continuous nature of these methods helps optimization, the stochastic sampling required for rendering is costly and can result in noise."
Strong critical analysis identifying speed-quality trade-off as central challenge.
Move 3 & 4: Gaps & Synthesis
Gaps: Speed-quality trade-off, real-time rendering at high resolution, MVS dependency
"Point-based α-blending and NeRF-style volumetric rendering share essentially the same image formation model."
Mathematical equivalence established as foundation for approach.
↓
Section 3: Method ~2000 words
3.1: 3D Gaussian Representation
Key Points: Scene representation with 3D Gaussians, initialization from SfM points, preserves volumetric properties while avoiding empty space computation
Strong technical foundation with practical initialization strategy.
3.2: Optimization
Key Points: Interleaved optimization/density control, adaptive Gaussian management, anisotropic covariance optimization
Clear algorithmic procedure with adaptive strategy for representation quality.
3.3: Fast Differentiable Rendering
Key Points: Tile-based rasterization, visibility-aware rendering, anisotropic splatting, GPU-optimized
Emphasis on real-time performance with GPU efficiency for both forward and backward passes.
↓
Section 4: Experiments ~1200 words
Empirical Validation
Metrics: PSNR, SSIM, training time, rendering FPS
Datasets: NeRF synthetic, Mip-NeRF360, Tanks and Temples, Deep Blending
Baselines: Mip-NeRF360, InstantNGP, Plenoxels
Comprehensive evaluation across multiple established benchmarks with quantitative performance metrics.

Synthesis & Impact
Contributions: Three key technical elements restated
Limitations: Representation size, memory requirements
Future Work: Real-time editing, dynamic scenes
Applications: VR/AR, real-time scene capture
Balanced discussion with transparent limitations and clear future directions, connected to broader applications.

Imitation Framework for Future Papers

Structural Elements to Adapt:

  • Enumerated Contributions: Clear numbered list of key technical elements
  • Quantitative Gap Statements: Specific performance targets (e.g., "≥ 30 fps at 1080p")
  • Multi-Dataset Evaluation: Comprehensive comparison across established benchmarks
  • Open Science: Code availability and reproducibility emphasized

Rhetorical Strategies:

  • Problem-Solution Structure: Clear gap identification followed by enumerated solutions
  • Technical Positioning: Mathematical connections between paradigms as foundation
  • Performance Emphasis: Quantifiable outcomes that directly address identified gaps
  • Practical Relevance: Real-world constraints and applications emphasized

Quality Indicators:

  • Mathematical Rigor: Formal definitions and algorithmic descriptions
  • Empirical Validation: Multiple evaluation metrics and comprehensive baselines
  • Transparency: Open-source code and detailed implementation
  • Practical Performance: Real-world constraints (resolution, speed) as key metrics
